2023 Revaluation Retrospective — Lessons for 2028

42% Average Increase

The 2023 revaluation saw a 42% average increase in assessed values — one of the largest in Craven County history. This drove significant public concern and appeal volume.

Waterfront Disparity

Waterfront properties (New Bern, River Bend, Trent Woods) saw 50-70% increases while inland rural properties saw 20-30%. This created equity concerns and concentrated appeals in waterfront areas.

Predictive Opportunity

With 2 years of post-revaluation sales data, Craven County can now build predictive models for 2028 — identifying which neighborhoods will see the largest changes and proactively communicating with affected property owners.

What This Dashboard Demonstrates

Craven County's 2023 revaluation resulted in a 42% average increase — generating significant public concern and appeal volume. The 2028 revaluation will be even more complex with post-pandemic market shifts, waterfront premium dynamics, and Cherry Point's economic influence. This concept dashboard shows how predictive analytics can help the Tax Department model scenarios, identify equity impacts across 8 municipalities, forecast appeal volumes, and give the Board of Commissioners the data they need to set a revenue-neutral rate that's defensible and transparent.
