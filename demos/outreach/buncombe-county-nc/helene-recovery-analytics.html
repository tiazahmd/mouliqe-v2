Data Collection Burden

Per project, HUD requires:

  • - Beneficiary demographics (race, income, disability)
  • - National objective documentation (LMI, urgent need, slum/blight)
  • - Environmental review records (NEPA)
  • - Procurement documentation (competitive bidding)
  • - Davis-Bacon wage compliance (construction)
  • - Section 3 hiring/contracting reports
  • - Duplication of Benefits (DOB) analysis

Current Gap Assessment

114 projects across 7 jurisdictions, each with different data systems. Tetra Tech handles PPDR intake but no analytics vendor identified for cross-project tracking, compliance dashboards, or automated DRGR reporting. Kevin Madsen's Recovery Office is 18 months old — still building systems.

Mouliqe Fit

Unified analytics layer across all 7 jurisdictions — project tracking, compliance monitoring, automated QPR generation, and real-time dashboards for the Recovery Officer and Board of Commissioners.

What This Dashboard Demonstrates

Buncombe County's Helene recovery is one of the largest disaster recovery programs in North Carolina history — 114 projects, 7 jurisdictions, $1.4B+ in federal funding, and HUD CDBG-DR compliance requirements that demand granular tracking of every dollar, beneficiary, and outcome. This concept dashboard shows how a unified analytics platform can give Recovery Officer Kevin Madsen and the Strategy & Innovation team real-time visibility across all projects, automate the quarterly performance reports HUD requires, and ensure the county stays ahead of compliance requirements rather than scrambling at reporting deadlines.
