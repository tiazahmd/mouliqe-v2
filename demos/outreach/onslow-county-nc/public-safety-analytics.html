What This Dashboard Demonstrates

Onslow County's opioid crisis is driven by unique factors — Camp Lejeune's transient population, Jacksonville's ranking as a substance abuse hotspot, and $19M in settlement funds that require NC DOJ compliance reporting. Today, overdose data lives in EMS records, arrest data in the Sheriff's RMS, diversion outcomes in the COSS program files, and settlement spending in Finance spreadsheets. This concept dashboard shows how integrating these data streams creates the unified picture needed for evidence-based intervention, grant compliance, and the outcome metrics that justify continued settlement fund allocation.
