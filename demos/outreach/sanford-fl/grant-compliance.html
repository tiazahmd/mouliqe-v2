Spotlight: FEMA Marina Appeal

$18.2M Requested vs. $6.4M FEMA Estimate — $11.8M Gap

The Gap

FEMA estimated $6.4M for marina restoration. Sanford's engineering assessment shows $18.2M needed. The $11.8M difference requires detailed documentation of scope, damage assessment, and cost justification.

What's Needed

Itemized cost breakdown, engineering reports, damage documentation, comparable project costs, and a compliance-ready appeal package. All must meet FEMA Public Assistance guidelines.

How Analytics Helps

Automated cost tracking, milestone documentation, and compliance reporting that builds the evidence package FEMA requires — reducing the risk of appeal denial.

Sanford manages 12 active grants across 6 federal agencies with no grant management platform.

Finance handles Grant Compliance alongside Accounting, Treasury, Budgeting, Purchasing, and IT. Automated tracking frees Cynthia Lindsay's team to focus on the $18.2M FEMA appeal.
