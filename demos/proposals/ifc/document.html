Executive summary

IFC's advisory teams produce pitch decks for every buy-side engagement. Each deck requires deep research across company financials, industry dynamics, country macro conditions, and IFC's own track record — then structuring that research into a presentation-ready format. The work is thorough, high-quality, and essential. It is also repetitive, time-intensive, and follows a consistent pattern that is well-suited to AI automation.

This document proposes an AI-powered engine that automates the research and assembly phases of pitch deck creation. The engine takes a company name, country, and sector as input, executes deep research across public sources, evaluates the quality of that research against IFC standards, and generates a structured pitch deck ready for analyst review and refinement.

A working prototype is live today. It generates complete pitch decks end-to-end in under 15 minutes — work that currently takes analysts 8 to 12 hours. The output is a first draft, not a final product. The analyst remains in control: reviewing, editing, adding proprietary context, and making the final call on what goes to the client.

Critically, the engine operates entirely outside IFC's security perimeter. It requires no integration with IFC internal systems, no access to proprietary databases, and no connection to IFC infrastructure. All inputs are public information. All research comes from public sources. The tool runs like any external research application an analyst might use in a browser.

Guiding principles

Analyst-in-the-loop

The engine produces a first draft. The analyst reviews, refines, and decides what goes to the client. AI handles the research grunt work, not the judgment.

Zero proprietary data

No IFC internal documents, deal data, or classified information enters the system. All inputs are public. All research is from public sources.

Working demo first

No 6-month build cycle before anyone sees a result. The core pipeline is live. Enhancements are scoped. The roadmap is driven by analyst feedback.

Purpose-built for IFC

Not a generic AI deck generator. The research domains, deck structure, quality standards, and institutional voice are all IFC-specific.

The problem

Every buy-side advisory engagement at IFC begins with a pitch deck. The deck serves as the primary vehicle for presenting a target company to the advisory team and, ultimately, to the client. It must be comprehensive, well-sourced, and formatted to IFC's institutional standards.

Building a pitch deck is a multi-step process. The analyst researches the target company across multiple domains — corporate profile, ownership structure, financial performance, industry landscape, country macro conditions, and IFC's own track record in the relevant sector and region. Each domain requires searching multiple sources, cross-referencing data, and evaluating credibility. Once the research is complete, the analyst structures it into a slide deck, formats charts and tables, adds IFC boilerplate content, and reviews for consistency.

This process takes 8 to 12 hours per deck. The research methodology is well-defined. The slide structure is standardized. The quality bar is high but the pattern is predictable. Analysts follow the same sequence of steps for every engagement — the variables are the company, the sector, and the country, not the process itself.

The bottleneck is not analyst skill. It is analyst time. Senior professionals spend hours on research and assembly work that follows a consistent, repeatable pattern. Every hour spent on a pitch deck is an hour not spent on higher-value advisory work — client relationships, deal structuring, strategic analysis.

Current process
Step 1 — Research

Search company websites, financial databases, Bloomberg, Reuters, World Bank data, IFC.org, and sector reports. Gather data across 5+ domains. Cross-reference sources for accuracy. Flag gaps and contradictions. 4-6 hours.

Step 2 — Assembly

Structure research into slide format. Write executive summary. Build financial tables and charts. Add IFC boilerplate (disclaimer, WBG overview, capabilities). Format to IFC template standards. 2-4 hours.

Step 3 — Review

Check data consistency across slides. Verify source citations. Ensure institutional tone and formatting standards. Iterate with team feedback. 1-2 hours.

The engine automates Steps 1 and 2 — the research and assembly — and produces a first draft for Step 3. The analyst's review and refinement remains human-driven.

How it works

The engine is a three-phase AI pipeline that mirrors the analyst's research process. Each phase is purpose-built, not a generic AI wrapper. The pipeline uses multiple specialized AI models — one for orchestration and evaluation, one for deep web research, and one for presentation rendering.

Phase 1 — Deep Research

Automated research across public sources

The engine analyzes the analyst's input (company name, country, sector, optional supporting documents) and builds a targeted research brief. It then executes deep web research across company profile, financial performance, industry dynamics, country macro conditions, and IFC track record. Sources include company IR pages, financial databases, World Bank publications, IFC.org public pages, news outlets, and sector reports.

Technology: Perplexity sonar-deep-research for web research, Claude for orchestration

Phase 2 — Quality Evaluation

Automated quality control with retry logic

Research results are evaluated against IFC-calibrated quality standards. The evaluation checks source credibility, data recency, completeness across all required domains, and whether there is sufficient depth to produce a credible pitch deck. If gaps are found, the engine automatically rewrites the research brief with a different strategy and retries — up to three attempts — until quality thresholds are met or the system reports what could not be found.

Technology: Claude with extended thinking for evaluation and decision-making

Phase 3 — Deck Generation

Structured pitch deck output

Verified research is structured into a presentation-ready pitch deck following IFC's standard format. The engine determines the appropriate sections and depth based on the research output and engagement type. Each slide includes sourced data points, financial figures where available, and IFC boilerplate content. The output is a downloadable PPTX file.

Technology: Claude for content structuring, Gamma for PPTX rendering

Real-time progress

The entire pipeline streams progress in real time. The analyst sees each phase as it executes — research brief construction, deep research with source counts, quality evaluation decisions, retry attempts if needed, and deck generation. Total processing time is typically under 15 minutes.

File upload support

Analysts can optionally upload supporting documents (PDF, DOCX) — public reports such as Fitch ratings, Economist articles, or sector analyses. The engine extracts content from these documents and incorporates it into the research brief, giving the AI additional context for more targeted research.

What it produces

The engine generates a structured pitch deck tailored to the target company, sector, and engagement type. The number of slides and depth of each section adapts to the research output — well-documented public companies produce richer decks; less-documented private companies produce focused decks with clearly flagged data gaps.

The standard output includes the following sections, though the engine adds or removes content based on what the research surfaces:

Data privacy & security

The engine is designed to operate entirely outside IFC's security perimeter. This is not a workaround — it is a deliberate architectural decision that eliminates the need for IT security review, systems integration, or network access approvals.

Security Architecture
✓
No IFC systems integration
The engine is a standalone web application. It does not connect to, access, or require any IFC internal systems, databases, or infrastructure. No SSO, no VPN, no network access. It runs like any external research tool an analyst might use — no different from accessing Bloomberg or Google in a browser.
✓
No proprietary data as input
The only required inputs are public information: company name, country, and sector. Optional file uploads are for public reports — Fitch ratings, Economist articles, sector analyses. No IFC internal documents, deal data, client information, or classified material enters the system at any point.
✓
All research from public sources
The research engine searches the open web — company websites, financial databases, World Bank publications, IFC.org public pages, news outlets, and sector reports. These are the same sources an analyst would access through a browser. No proprietary databases, no internal IFC data, no restricted information.
✓
No data retention
Research sessions are ephemeral. No company data, research results, or generated decks are stored on the platform after the session ends. The analyst downloads the output and the session data is discarded. There is no persistent database of past research or generated content.
✓
No AppSec review required
Because the engine does not touch IFC's network, does not process proprietary data, and does not integrate with internal systems, it falls outside the scope of IT security review. It is an external tool used by analysts at their discretion — the same category as any web-based research service.

This architecture is intentional. The most common barrier to AI tool adoption in large institutions is the security review process. By designing the engine to require zero integration and zero proprietary data, that barrier is eliminated entirely.

Roadmap

The working demo represents the core pipeline — Phase 1. Production enhancements are scoped and ready to build. The roadmap is structured around analyst feedback: each phase delivers working functionality, and priorities are adjusted based on what analysts actually need.

PHASE 1 Core Pipeline
Complete

Deep research across 5 domains, quality evaluation with intelligent retry logic, and full pitch deck generation. Working demo deployed and generating decks today. Real-time progress streaming. File upload support for supplementary documents.

Demo: ifc-demo-frontend-v73g.vercel.app

PHASE 2 Production Enhancements
Scoped

Branded IFC templates — pixel-perfect PPTX output matching IFC's template standards, colors (navy #021F43, sky blue #00B0F0), and fonts (Calibri Light/Calibri). Boilerplate slides (cover, disclaimer, section dividers, contacts) rendered from the IFC master template.

Research verification layer — a specialized verification agent that cross-references key data points across multiple sources, flags contradictions, checks data recency, and produces a confidence assessment per section. Designed to catch errors before the analyst sees them.

Multi-engagement-type support — extend beyond buy-side advisory to capital raise, sell-side advisory, and pre-IPO engagement types. Each type has its own slide schema, research requirements, and boilerplate content.

Analyst feedback loops — structured mechanism for analysts to flag issues, suggest improvements, and rate output quality. Feedback directly improves the prompts and pipeline with each iteration.

PHASE 3 Scale
Planned

Multi-user support with usage analytics. Prompt library for different engagement types and regional variations. Integration with analyst workflows — the tool becomes part of the standard engagement kickoff process. Built to handle the full advisory team's volume.
