Executive summary

bKash has built the largest mobile financial services network in Bangladesh — 82 million verified customers, over 1 million merchants, and 360,000 agent points. The infrastructure for digital payments exists at extraordinary scale. The challenge is no longer reach. It is conversion: turning the presence of bKash at a merchant location into an active payment at the point of sale.

bKash knows transaction volumes per merchant. It does not know why some locations convert at high rates and others do not. The answer is in the physical environment — where the QR code sits, whether customers notice the signage, how the layout of the counter shapes the moment of payment decision. That behavioral data does not exist today. Placement of QR codes, signage, and promotional materials is guided by general best practices and field representative judgment, not by measured evidence of what actually captures customer attention.

This document proposes a three-phase initiative that closes this gap and transforms it into a scalable competitive advantage:

Phase 1 — The Pilot

Install cameras at 50–100 merchant and agent locations across diverse formats. Capture rich customer behavior data — foot traffic, dwell time, gaze direction, QR code visibility, signage attention, queue dynamics — using an AI-powered computer vision pipeline. Build the labeled dataset that maps physical layouts to digital payment behavior. All processing happens on-site; raw footage never leaves the location.

Phase 2 — The Model

Use the pilot data to develop a proprietary algorithm that predicts optimal QR code, signage, and branding placement based on location geometry, layout type, and customer interaction patterns. The model learns what the cameras observed — which configurations drive the highest digital payment engagement.

Phase 3 — The Tool

A standalone tool for field teams. Upload a photo of any merchant or agent location, answer a few questions about dimensions and layout, and the system outputs ideal placement recommendations for QR codes, signage, and promotional materials. No cameras. No on-site hardware. Placement optimization scaled across the entire 1M+ merchant and 360K+ agent network.

The platform is delivered through a strategic partnership between Mouliqe (AI architecture, computer vision, model development, and product engineering) and Parlance Consulting Services Ltd. (market research methodology, field operations, location relationships, and regulatory compliance).

Guiding principles

Privacy by architecture

Raw video never enters the data pipeline. Anonymization at the edge. The central platform only receives structured, non-identifiable data.

Pilot as investment

Phase 1 is not just data collection — it is training data acquisition. Every design decision is made with Phase 2 model development in mind.

Research-grade, honestly framed

Camera-based gaze estimation is directional intelligence, not lab precision. Accuracy reported transparently and calibrated against ground-truth.

Built to scale

Phase 3 eliminates cameras entirely. The tool works from a photo and a questionnaire. Every location in the network gets data-driven placement guidance.

The opportunity

Bangladesh's mobile financial services sector has grown at extraordinary pace. With over 200 million MFS accounts nationwide and bKash commanding the dominant market position, the infrastructure for digital payments is in place. The next growth frontier is not adding more merchants to the network — it is increasing the rate at which existing merchants convert cash transactions to digital payments.

The gap between merchant network size and merchant payment adoption is well documented. The majority of transactions at bKash merchant locations remain cash-based. Customers who have bKash accounts and are standing at a bKash-enabled merchant still default to cash. The question is why — and the answer is not in the app, the pricing, or the merchant's willingness. It is in the physical environment.

Does the customer see the QR code? Is the signage positioned where attention naturally falls? Does the layout of the counter create a natural moment for scanning? Is the bKash branding visible during the queue — the window when payment method decisions are actually made? These questions cannot be answered by transaction data alone. They require behavioral data from the physical point of interaction.

That data does not exist today. QR code placement, signage positioning, and branding material deployment are guided by general best practices and field representative judgment. There is no systematic measurement of what customers actually see, what they engage with, and what they ignore. The gap between what is spent on merchant-level branding and what is known about its effectiveness is the opportunity this initiative addresses.

The platform extracts a full spectrum of customer behavior metrics from standard camera footage at merchant and agent locations. Each metric feeds both the Phase 1 analytics dashboards and the Phase 2 model training pipeline.

QR Code Visibility

Signage & Branding Attention

Queue Behavior

Transaction Point Interaction

Phone usage, cash handling vs. scanning behavior, merchant-customer exchange dynamics.

Demographic Segments

Anonymous age-range and gender estimation. Which segments are most receptive to digital payment prompts.

Temporal Patterns

Behavioral variation by time of day and week. Peak windows for digital payment receptivity.

Layout–Conversion Mapping

The key Phase 2 input: correlating layout, QR placement, and signage with payment behavior.

The vision — three phases

The initiative is structured as three sequential phases, each building on the outputs of the previous one. Phase 1 produces the data. Phase 2 produces the intelligence. Phase 3 produces the tool.

Phase 1 — The Pilot
Data Collection & Behavioral Analytics

Install cameras at 50–100 merchant and agent locations across diverse formats — from small roadside agent points to larger retail merchants. Run the computer vision pipeline to capture foot traffic, dwell time, gaze direction, QR code visibility, signage attention, queue dynamics, and transaction point interaction. All processing happens on-site using portable hardware — raw footage never leaves the location. The output is twofold: (1) analytics dashboards and research reports for immediate business value, and (2) a labeled dataset mapping physical layouts to measured payment behavior — the training data for Phase 2.

Output: behavioral analytics + labeled training dataset

Phase 2 — The Model
Proprietary Placement Optimization Algorithm

Train a proprietary algorithm on the Phase 1 dataset. The model learns the relationship between location geometry (dimensions, layout type, counter position, queue area) and digital payment behavior (QR code attention, signage engagement, payment method choice). The output is a predictive model that takes location layout parameters as input and outputs optimal placement recommendations for QR codes, signage, and promotional materials — without needing cameras or on-site observation.

Output: placement optimization algorithm (the core IP)

Phase 3 — The Tool
Scalable Placement Optimization for the Network

Package the model into a standalone tool for field teams. A field representative uploads a photo of any merchant or agent location, answers a few questions (square footage, layout type, daily footfall, transaction volume), and the system outputs a visual placement recommendation map for QR codes, signage, and promotional materials. No cameras. No installation. No per-location hardware. Placement optimization scaled to every location in the 1M+ merchant and 360K+ agent network.

Output: scalable placement tool for the entire network

Continuous improvement

The model improves with scale. Every new location format and every new geographic region adds training data. As the network expands, the algorithm becomes more accurate — delivering better placement recommendations with each new merchant or agent point onboarded.

Phase 2 — the model

The pilot data becomes the training set for a proprietary placement optimization algorithm. Phase 2 transforms observational data into predictive intelligence — a model that can recommend optimal QR code, signage, and branding placement without needing cameras or on-site observation.

What goes in

Location geometry

Dimensions, layout type, counter position, queue area, entry/exit points, existing signage locations. Captured from location documentation and camera-derived spatial analysis.

Measured behavior data

QR code attention scores, signage dwell times, gaze direction vectors, queue behavior patterns, transaction point interaction events, demographic-segmented behavior. Captured from the Phase 1 CV pipeline across 50–100 locations.

What the model learns

QR position → scan probability

Which QR code positions maximize the likelihood that a customer notices and scans. How counter proximity, height, and angle affect visibility.

Signage → payment method choice

How signage placement affects whether customers choose bKash over cash. Which positions influence the decision moment.

Queue time → conversion window

How queue duration and signage placement during wait time correlate with digital payment adoption. The queue is an untapped influence window.

Format generalization

Patterns that hold across location formats — from small agent kiosks to large merchant shops. Which principles are universal and which are format-specific.

What comes out

A predictive algorithm that takes location layout parameters as input and outputs optimal placement recommendations for QR codes, signage, and promotional materials — without needing cameras, on-site hardware, or behavioral observation. The model encodes what the cameras learned.

Why it improves over time

Every new location and every new layout format adds training data. The model becomes more accurate as the network expands — delivering better recommendations with each new merchant or agent point onboarded.

Phase 3 — the tool

Phase 3 packages the proprietary model into a standalone tool that eliminates the need for cameras, on-site hardware, and per-location installation. The tool makes placement optimization accessible to every merchant and agent location in the network — at the cost of uploading a photo and answering a few questions.

How it works
Step 1 — Upload a photo

A field representative takes a photo of the merchant or agent location. The system uses computer vision to analyze the spatial layout — identifying counter positions, display areas, queue zones, and existing signage locations.

Step 2 — Answer a few questions

Location square footage, layout type, average daily footfall, transaction volume, and any placement constraints. A simple form designed for field representatives — no technical knowledge required.

Step 3 — Get placement recommendations

The model outputs a visual placement map: where to position QR codes, signage, and promotional materials for maximum customer attention and digital payment conversion. Recommendations are ranked by predicted impact and annotated with confidence scores. The output is immediately actionable — a field representative can implement the recommendations on the spot.

Scaling across the network

The pilot covers 50–100 locations with cameras. The tool covers every location without them. With over 1 million merchants and 360,000 agent points, the ability to optimize placement at scale — without per-location camera installation — transforms how bKash drives digital payment adoption across the entire network.

Agent points

Small-format cash-in/cash-out locations where counter space is limited and every placement decision has outsized impact on visibility.

Small merchants

Roadside shops and kiosks where bKash competes with cash at the moment of payment. QR visibility is the conversion lever.

Retail merchants

Larger shops with multiple product zones, counter displays, and queue areas creating multiple touchpoints for bKash branding.

New markets & regions

As the network expands geographically, the model adapts to new location formats and regional patterns without starting from scratch.

The end state

Every field representative in the network can optimize QR and signage placement in minutes — without hiring consultants, installing cameras, or running observational studies. The tool does what used to require weeks of fieldwork. That is the value of Phase 3.
